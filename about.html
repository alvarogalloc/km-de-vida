The Problem

Food waste is a worldwide problem. Here in Guadalajara there is up to a 25% waste across the whole food production chain. That means that a quarter of all the food that we produce (grow, process and distribute) never even reaches people’s plates.

On top of that, perfectly edible meals also end up in the trash because of overproduction, severe quality standards and other inefficiencies in places such as restaurants, hotels, supermarkets. Even in our homes we contribute to that problem. Households are the main source of food waste in Mexico, caused by reasons such as overbuying, poor storage, or not checking expiration dates.

On the other hand, foster homes, shelters, orphanages, and similar institutions frequently struggle to provide enough meals for children, the homeless, and other vulnerable people.

As we can see, the problem is not the lack of food, but rather the poor logistics to distribute it efficiently. We have in our hands a huge opportunity to build a solution that can help reduce both problems.

Why Kilómetros de Vida?

This idea came to mind when trying to choose a project where a web application would aid in fulfilling at least one of the UN’s Sustainable Development Goals. While focusing on the needs of our own community in Guadalajara, the UN’s second goal of Zero Hunger immediately came to mind.

According to a 2020 study by the Secretaría de Hacienda y Crédito Público, 23.3% of Guadalajara’s metropolitan population lives in moderate poverty, and 1.53% lives in extreme poverty. That means that over 1,200,000 people lack food security. Enough food is produced in Guadalajara to feed its entire population — the problem lies in distribution, which leads to waste. This connects directly to SDG 12: Responsible Consumption and Production.

Everyone in our university is aware that we are among the privileged few with access to private education. Most of us have not and will never face the challenges we describe, but we have observed that many people want to help, they just don’t know how.

This website aims to bridge that gap: connecting volunteers with organizations and donations, helping move food from point A to point B — food that would otherwise go to waste. All that is needed is a car and time.
